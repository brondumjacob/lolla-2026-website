The Complete Genre Guide to Lollapalooza 2026

Fourteen genres, 172 artists, one weekend · July 2026

Lollapalooza's lineup isn't one festival, it's fourteen overlapping ones. Sort the 172-artist bill by genre and the range gets a lot more specific: 35 indie acts, 26 pop artists, 25 rock bands, 20 electronic producers, and pockets of K-Pop, J-Pop, country, and even a youth orchestra. Here's what's actually on the bill in every corner of the genre map.

Pop & Alt Pop (30 artists)

Pop is the single largest tier at Lolla 2026, and it splits into two headlining lanes. Charli XCX headlines Friday off the back of brat, and Tate McRae closes the whole festival Sunday night. Around them, the major tier carries real depth — Muna, Jade, Audrey Hobert, and Zara Larsson all hold major slots, while Sunday's undercard alone stacks Adéla, Sunshine, and Cruz Beckham and the Breakers. The four-artist Alt Pop pocket is worth a special mention: Not for Radio plays a major slot Friday, while Between Friends, Ella Red, and Pearly Drops round out the undercard with a moodier, guitar-inflected take on pop songwriting.

Rock (25 artists)

The Smashing Pumpkins headline Friday, giving Lolla's deepest rock lineup in years a genuine flagship. Yungblud and 5 Seconds of Summer anchor the majors alongside Turnstile and Viagra Boys, and the undercard runs from hardcore-adjacent acts like Loathe and High Vis to melodic rock from Wolf Alice and Wunderhorse. Hot Mulligan brings the emo revival to a major slot Sunday, closing out a tier that spans several decades of guitar music without repeating itself once.

Deep Cut

Wolf Alice (Rock, Undercard, Sat) is one of the most decorated bands on the entire bill relative to its billing — a Mercury Prize winner playing a stage well below its actual draw.

Indie (35 artists)

Indie is the biggest single genre on the bill, carried by two very different headline-adjacent acts: Ethel Cain's gothic Americana on Saturday and The XX's minimalist co-headline Sunday. The major tier is stacked — Geese, The Neighbourhood, Beabadoobee, Wet Leg, and Suki Waterhouse all play major slots spread across every day of the festival. The undercard is where the tier goes deepest: Horsegirl and Water From Your Eyes chase noisier, more experimental territory, while Finn Wolfhard and CMAT sit closer to straightforward guitar pop.

Electronic & EDM (38 artists combined)

Split across two adjacent genre tags, electronic and EDM together make up the second-largest chunk of the lineup. John Summit headlines Thursday off a house scene built in Chicago, and the majors run deep with Alison Wonderland, Major Lazer, Empire of the Sun, and Kettama. Boris Brejcha's high-tech minimal and Snow Strippers' hyperpop-leaning sets round out Thursday's major slots. In the undercard, names like Oklou, Ninajirachi, and Whethan are the ones worth catching early if you want to say you saw them before the stages got bigger.

Hip-Hop & R&B (26 artists combined)

Clipse's major-stage reunion and Freddie Gibbs anchor the hip-hop side of the bill, both playing to rap purists who want technical writing over spectacle. Lil Uzi Vert and Mustard bring the genre's more maximalist, hit-driven register to Friday's major slots. R&B centers on Olivia Dean's Saturday headline set, with Blood Orange and Leon Thomas holding down major slots and Amber Mark, Destin Conrad, and Justine Skye carrying the undercard's vocal-forward end of the tier.

K-Pop, J-Pop & the Rest of the World (7 artists)

Jennie's Saturday headline set brings K-Pop to the very top of the bill, and she's joined by I-DLE and Aespa in the majors — genuine arena-level production values on a festival stage. SB19 and Cortis carry the undercard. J-Pop gets its own foothold with Ado and Yoasobi both playing major slots Sunday, giving the festival's most internationally-minded corner two of its strongest sets of the weekend.

Folk & Country (9 artists combined)

Both genres live almost entirely in the undercard this year, which makes them some of the lineup's best discovery bets. Paris Paloma's viral folk anthem "labour" carries real festival-crowd energy despite an early Thursday slot, and Kingfishr and Amble round out a strong folk pocket. Country is thinner but sharp: Calder Allen, Cameron Whitcomb, Elizabeth Nichols, and Waylon Wyatt spread country slots across three different days.

The One-Of-A-Kind Slots

Two tags on the bill don't fit anywhere else. The Chicago Youth Symphony Orchestra plays Saturday under the Classical tag — a genuinely unusual booking for a festival built on rock and electronic music, and a nod to the festival's hometown. Chicago Made, tagged Various, plays Friday as a showcase built around the city itself rather than any single genre.

The Bottom Line

Sort Lolla 2026 by day, and you get four strong lineups. Sort it by genre, and you get fourteen different festivals happening in the same park. That's the real value of a bill this size — whatever you came for, it's here, and there's a good chance the artist two stages over is playing something you didn't know you wanted to hear.
